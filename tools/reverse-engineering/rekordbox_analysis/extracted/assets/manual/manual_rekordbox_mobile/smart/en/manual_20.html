Hot Cues

By storing cue and loop points as hot cues, the points can be cued and regular or loop playback can be started at the touch of a button. Up to three hot cues (A, B and C) can be stored per music file. (* For XDJ-RX, up to four hot cues can be stored.)

When a point is stored as a hot cue after turning the quantize function on, the nearest beat position on the beat grid is stored as the hot cue. (Beat Snap)

1Play the track on the player panel.

2When [HOT CUE (A)], [HOT CUE (B)] or [HOT CUE (C)] is tapped, the currently playing point (or the point where playback is currently paused) is stored at the corresponding hot cue button.

When [HOT CUE (A) – (C)] is tapped during loop playback, the loop points are stored as the hot cue.

Notes

[HOT CUE (A) – (C)] are highlighted in green when cues are stored at them, in orange when loops are stored at them.

If a cue or loop is already stored at [HOT CUE (A) – (C)], delete the hot cue stored at the corresponding hot cue button before stored a new cue or loop.

Playing hot cues

Starting playback from a stored point

When [HOT CUE (A)], [HOT CUE (B)] or [HOT CUE (C)] is tapped while a track is playing, the playing position jumps to the point saved at that button and playback starts from there.

Deleting stored points

When [] at the right of [HOT CUE (A)], [HOT CUE (B)] or [HOT CUE (C)] is tapped, the point stored at the corresponding hot cue button is deleted.

DJ Performances

Performing with your CDJ/XDJ unit(s)

Applicable models: CDJ-2000NXS/CDJ-900NXS/XDJ-1000

Once the link is established between the mobile device and DJ players, first check the order of the DJ player icons displayed in rekordbox (iOS/Android) and arrange them according to the actual layout of the DJ players.

1Establish the link between rekordbox (iOS/Android) and the DJ players.

  • Linking with CDJ/XDJ Units

When the link is established, the browser panel opens.

2When [Settings] is tapped on the browser panel’s toolbar, a list of the setting items is displayed.

3Tap [Arrangement of icons of CDJ/XDJ].

A panel for changing the order of the icons opens.

4Drag the DJ players’ icons to change their order.

  • For instructions on checking the player numbers displayed at the DJ player icons, see the operating instructions of the respective DJ players.

5Tap [Back] to finish changing the order of the icons.

Reflecting the “My Settings” values on the DJ units

A DJ player’s main unit settings can be saved in rekordbox (iOS/Android) and these same settings can be applied to the respective DJ players. This lets users immediately set up DJ players with the settings they are used to so they can devote themselves to DJ performances at their ease.

  • My Settings

1When [Settings] is tapped on the browser panel’s toolbar, a list of the setting items is displayed.

2When [My Settings] is tapped, the items that can be set at “My Settings” are displayed.

3When [Remote setting using “Pro DJ Link”] is tapped, a panel for selecting the DJ player(s) to which the settings are to apply opens.

4Stop playback on the DJ player on which you want to reflect the settings.

5When a DJ player’s icon is tapped, the “My Settings” values are reflected on the DJ player.

Loading music files onto DJ units

Music files can be selected on the browser panel and loaded onto DJ players.

1When a track is tapped on the browser panel, the link panel (CDJs/XDJs) opens.

Playlists and taglists can be used to organize music files for convenience when selecting tracks.

  • Organizing Music in Playlists

  • Selecting Music for a Taglist

2When a DJ player’s icon is tapped, the music file is loaded into that DJ player.

Storing hot cues in a DJ player

  • Hot cues stored in loaded music files can be stored as a DJ player’s hot cues (A, B and C). For instructions on storing and calling out (REC/CALL) hot cues on the DJ player, see the respective DJ player’s operating instructions.

  • When [Automatic load of Hot Cues to CDJ/XDJ] is set, the DJ player’s hot cues (A, B and C) can also be forcibly replaced with the hot cues stored in loaded music files.
